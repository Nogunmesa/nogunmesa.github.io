What is Identity?

Identity is a complex and dynamic concept. It includes the labels we assign ourselves and those that society assigns to us. Identity is shaped by various factors, such as race, ethnicity, gender, class, sexual identity, and ability.

Understanding Intersectionality

Intersectionality refers to the way different aspects of our identity combine to create unique experiences of privilege and discrimination.

Case Study: Combahee River Collective

The Combahee River Collective was a group of Black feminists who advocated for both racial and gender equality, emphasizing the importance of addressing multiple forms of oppression.

Ethics, Cultural Competence, and Inclusion

Ethics involves making decisions that respect the dignity and rights of others. Cultural competence requires understanding diverse cultural backgrounds, and inclusion is about ensuring equity for all individuals.

Historical Trends & Organizational Culture

Historical events have shaped how organizations function and how technology has evolved. Understanding these trends is key to fostering inclusive organizational cultures.
